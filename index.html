about

I am a PhD candidate at the Stockholm Resilience Centre based at Stockholm University, and part of the FORMAS funded SAFER project Safeguarding Freshwater Resilience in the Anthropocene. The project aims to understand how severe and widespread anthropogenic changes to the global freshwater cycle impacts distant regions and countries through teleconnections, both bio-physical [moisture recycling], and socio-economic [virtual water trade]. My research is focussed on how we can quantify global scale freshwater resilience, identifying hotspots for water resilience loss, and better understanding the role of these teleconnections in propagating the effects of water resilience loss to distant regions. Using data from satellite remote sensing, my methods include statistical analysis, emperical dynamic modelling, and network analysis.
